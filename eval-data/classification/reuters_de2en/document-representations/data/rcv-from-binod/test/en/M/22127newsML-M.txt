spot electricity prices in the northeastern U.S. were steady to lower Thursday amid cooler-than-normal summer weather in the region , industry sources said . 
the latest weather forecasts indicated temperatures in the Northeast to be normal to above-normal next week . 
trading of daily power in the region was subdued ahead of the three-day U.S. Labor Day weekend , traders said . 
in the New York Power pool ( NYPP ) , peak electricity prices for next-day delivery were quoted at $ 23.00- $ 24.00 per megawatt hour ( MWh ) , near unchanged from earlier this week . 
Next-day , off-peak power in NYPP was assessed at $ 14.00- $ 16.00 , down from $ 18.00- $ 22.50 earlier this week . 
in the New England Power pool ( NEPOOL ) , peak power for Friday delivery was discussed at $ 24.00- $ 25.00 , down from the $ 25.00- $ 27.00 range for the early part of the week . 
Next-day NEPOOL prices for off-peak hours were pegged at $ 17.00- $ 18.00 , compared with $ 18.00- $ 22.50 earlier this week .
